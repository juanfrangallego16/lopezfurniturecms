WHAT IS THE BEST STYLE FOR YOUR HOME?

When it is time to decorate your home, it is likely that questions will arise. This is because we may not be sure which style best suits us. But it may also be that we just don’t know how to furnish and decorate our homes.
Unquestionably, all interior designs have their key points: materials, colors, and many details that are characteristics of each one of them. In the same way, it is important to understand that with each decorative style certain pieces fit that will help you enhance the effect that defines it. Would you like to know more about styles? Let's discover what your favorite style is!

1.CLASSIC STYLE

The key to a classic interior is symmetry and order. The placement and style of your furniture is important. For example, setting up your living room decor facing a fireplace is one way. We associate the classic with what does not go out of style. If you like houses with high ceilings and elegant decor, that is part of the classic style. But even if your home doesn't have original moldings or chandeliers with high ceilings, you can have a classic style in your home.

Its indisputable timeless elegance is totally fashionable. It can adapt to the personality of those who will live in the home. This style is based on noble materials, in which baroque and rococo furniture stand out, luxurious floors, wall pieces, and a variety of accessories, but always with a balance that gives visual strength to the spaces.

2.MODERN STYLE

Today's modern style is based on a design movement that was born in the early 20th century. It is simple and unadorned and at the same time very elegant. In fact, the design of all furniture and decor must reflect its intended purpose. If a piece of furniture, decoration, or detail does not serve a practical purpose, then it can be removed.

In a house with this style, neutral colors will dominate. It is a style where lacquer covered wooden furniture and decor is very important, as are straight and pure lines.

3.RUSTIC STYLE

Beams, wood, stone... If these elements are your favorites for home decor, the rustic style is in your DNA.

The essence of the rustic style is simplicity, naturalness, and roughness. The furniture does not have too much ornamentation. There is a lack of modern materials such as metal, plastic, and synthetic fabrics. The predominant colors are brown, green, beige, gray, and gold tones. The feeling of artisan creation must be in each of the pieces of furniture and decorative details that you incorporate into your rustic interior design style. The environment has to be in harmony with nature.

The most important point is that this style can be combined with others.

4.MINIMALIST STYLE

Minimalist decor never goes out of style. This style is characterized by its simplicity. Minimalist homes are committed to "less is more" so that each space is functional, without neglecting style.

This is a style that stands in contrast to the colorful pop art style. Functional, futuristic, and balanced. Given that minimalism picks up the baton of architectural rationalism, these three premises that define rationalism can also be applied to the style that concerns us. A minimalist home has the essential elements, without sacrificing style, to provide maximum practicality to the home without overloading it. Since the homes of yesteryear tended to “exceed”, minimalism relates to the future precisely because it dispenses with it.

5.NORDIC STYLE

This style in recent years has become a trend as it is gaining a lot of strength over time. The origin of this type of decoration is found in Norway, Sweden, Finland, and Denmark, where sunny days are scarce for much of the year. A solution for replacing this lack of light was a need. The solution was to find light colors and some elements related to nature.

The ideal Nordic style comes from its focus on functionality, pragmatism, and clean aesthetics even with a minimalist bent. One of the things that I like the most about the Nordic style in interior decor is that it can be interpreted in many different ways, from a monochrome minimalist style to an eclectic style. The key is in the intermediate things and in the balance. The soft colors, pastel tones combined with old worn wood are important details in achieving a Nordic style in your home.

6.INDUSTRIAL STYLE

Interestingly enough, in the 1950s, young people looking for growth in big cities like New York or Chicago had to start in places that years ago had been factories, so we’re talking beams, bricks, and concrete buildings.

In this time, the industrial style was adopted, and today, interior designers are inclined towards these elements in the decor that proudly shows the materials that others try to hide.

The furniture used in this style of decor is made of metal or iron, even on some occasions it can be seen with a little rust on them. Untreated wood and veneers are also used.

An important point to counteract coldness or dark tones is a large amount of natural light. The industrial style references a flexible design that is inspired by warehouses, factories, and other industrial structures. Industrial-style decor is comprised of minimalist architectural details that include the use of bare brick, metal, and wood, as well as reclaimed and recycled materials.

7.BOHO CHIC STYLE

If you are a fan of the bohemian style, but its decorative exuberance stops you from adding it to your home, you can opt for boho chic. Find out what it has in common with Bohemians and why it's called chic.

The boho chic style definitely embraces the aesthetics of creative, carefree, and unconventional decoration. Its grace lies in the fact that it mixes different styles of decoration with a certain nonchalance.

If you look closely, few decorative styles are as cheerful, lively, and fun as the bohemian. It is a combination of vibrant colors, a fusion of cultures and eras, decorative freedom, and a mixture of styles. It is definitely a mix in which the hippy, the ethnic, and the oriental coexist, naturally creating a unique and very personal atmosphere.

I can honestly spend hours writing about the number of decorative styles that exist, like Mediterranean, nautica, farm house, and romantic style among many more. Without a doubt, however you choose to decorate your home will be a total success. The important thing is that you feel comfortable with your style of your preference.
