3 TIPS FOR DECORATING WITH A VINTAGE STYLE

The vintage style, if applied correctly with distinction, manages to capture the attention of even the most futuristic. Its warm tones and shapes with a touch of romanticism are the characteristic of this style.
Many will wonder where the term vintage comes from. We will gladly explain it to you. This term comes from the fusion of the French vingt (twenty) and age (years). Initially it was used to refer to the style inspired by the 1920s. However, today it is used to define, in general, all styles focused on decades like the 70s, 60s, and 50s.
The vintage style evokes nostalgia and sensitivity towards classic forms. In this style, handmade pieces that were once considered art are important.
You should also know that with the meeting of different elements, vintage offers a romantic environment in which colors such as white, ecru, gray and pastel tones merge.
You could say that vintage interior design is for treasure hunters, lovers of one-of-a-kind pieces, and patient collectors. There are those who say that the only limitation in vintage interior design is your imagination. It is a style that is now present in homes around the world. It would not be wrong to say that it is a new look of the retro style.
If you take a good look at it, vintage interior design is old and elegant. In this style, the use of antique or aged furniture and accessories is a must.
Keep in mind that the harmony of space and light should be elegant, but not overloaded. It is good to maintain smooth and flowing lines, carefully selected furniture, and decorative accessories.
Our recommendation is that if you want this style in your home, pay close attention to the small details that allow you to recreate this style when decorating your home.
If you want to give your home a change, here are three tips that will give a new look to your home.

1.COLORS FOR THE WALLS

Of all the chromatic range that can define the vintage style, pastel colors on the walls are favorites. They are beautiful, special, and delicate all at the same time. They have so much strength and are perfect so that every detail in this decoration stands out. If you want to give a vintage air to the living room, bedroom, or kitchen, opting for pastel colors on the walls is best.

Of course, you can also decorate the walls in vintage style with bright colors. However, most of the time it is better to opt for neutral tones and pastels, considering that they are much more romantic.

Blue, pink, or yellow combined with gray and powdery whites make a combination that is hard to resist. Without a doubt, this is a decoration idea that will quickly transport us to bygone times.

2.SELECTION OF FURNITURE

They say that there are pieces of furniture that speak. Of course, not literally! But something certain is that vintage pieces have so many stories to tell us and things to contribute to the decoration that we cannot stop listening to them.

Vintage furniture makes spaces more welcoming thanks to their presence because they have a special and unique appeal with history, with soul, that have stood the test of time and fashions.

If you buy some of this furniture from an antique dealer it will include data on its origin, such as the year and place from which it was taken. This information will lead you to have an idea of the treasure that you have taken home.

They provide a look with certain nostalgic notes, which makes you fall in love. If you are a lover of this type of style, between feminine and delicate, you cannot miss a good wardrobe, a four-poster bed, and/or a chest of memories in your home.

3.DECORATIVE ELEMENTS

There are those who prefer to buy original vintage pieces because they have a high economic value and are sometimes more aesthetic.

However, you can use any object that is old, either taken from an antique store or from that box of grandmother's relics that is usually at home.

The most important thing is to properly distribute them in your home to provide the desired style. Some of the suggested items are old edition books with leather covers, chandeliers or copper objects in shades of brown and gold, picture frames and pictures, an antique bicycle decorated with flowers, dimly lit ceiling lamps, mirrors decorated with flowers, small lights or worn wooden frames, 1940s or 1950s coffee tables, display cabinets instead of bookshelves, Chester model sofas, wooden trunks, rugs and cushions, flower vases, and any other rustic decor.

We know that time does not pass for everyone the same. As with wine, the decoration also has its great reserve that with the lustrums add beauty and uniqueness.

Even though in the vintage style the collection of many antiques is proposed, it is not convenient to abuse them. Over saturating the spaces will prevent the home from having a comfortable and harmonious environment. In part, vintage invites minimalism.

Vintage decoration is a style that has gained a lot of ground in homes around the world. It is a simple way to add more class and style to the spaces in your home.

In addition, it is a striking proposal to give old furniture another chance.
