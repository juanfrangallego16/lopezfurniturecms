5 TIPS TO HELP YOU AVOID ELECTRICAL ACCIDENTS AT HOME

It is necessary to create safety habits at home. It is important to bear in mind that this training should include the youngest of the home. You may wonder how can I include them? We encourage you to educate them about how dangerous electricity can become and teach them how to safely handle appliances, in this way you can avoid electrical accidents at home.
In order to prevent, it is also necessary to have knowledge, among other things, of what are the main causes of electrical accidents that can occur at home. Don't forget that it is your responsibility to take care of yourself and your family. Sometimes we think that the dangers are only outside our home and nothing bad can happen to us inside. But, oddly enough, most of the accidents worldwide happen inside ours home.
Despite this, we should not be alarmed, since many accidents can be prevented if we implement a few small safety measures at home. It is only a matter of considering certain aspects in order to prevent certain risks.
It is true that among the accidents that can occur in the home, electrical accidents are not the most frequent. In fact, they represent 1 in 10 domestic accidents, but they are considered among the most dangerous due to their high-risk nature. However, by following some basic guidelines, you can avoid unforeseen events that can turn into more serious problems.
Unfortunately, on many occasions it only takes a little oversight to end up shaken by the current. For this reason, prevention is the main measure to avoid electrical accidents in the home. Although it may not seem like it, a significant number of situations of this type are created at home.
A relevant point is to make sure that the electrical installation in the home is in optimal conditions. For this it is necessary to periodically check the cables and plugs that are distributed throughout the house.
The bathroom, kitchen, laundry room or any other place where electrical appliances, cables, and plugs exist are a potential risk if the necessary safety measures are not taken. What could be the reasons? These range from broken or exposed wires to faulty appliances that could cause a major electrical accident at home. That is why today we bring you some basic tips that will be very useful to prevent electrical accidents at home.

1.Electrical Cables

Avoid handling electrical cables without proper protection. If you need to touch an electrical cable it is recommended that you have dry clothes and hands, as well as it is good to keep in mind that it is better to wear shoes with rubber soles, insulating gloves. Among others, these are some important steps you can take to avoid an electric shock through your body.

2.Outlets

3.Unplug

It is important that small appliance hook ups are unplugged when they are not in use. When plugged in, even if not used, they consume a small amount of electricity.

Most small appliances have a pilot light or a timer that consumes electricity. For this reason, we recommend you get into the habit of unplugging them when they are not in use. This will help you see a decrease in your electricity bill each month. In addition, you will avoid major problems, in the event of a short circuit, electrical storms, etc.

Remember that you should never allow a plugged-in device to come in contact with water. In case a plug or power source gets wet, go to the switchboard and cut off the power flow to the entire home. Then, unplug the connected appliance and do not use the outlet again until it is completely dry.

4.Do not overload the outlets

As tempting as it may be to plug in or use multiple adapters to connect multiple electrical appliances at the same time, this is not recommended. This could trigger an electrical accident by overloading the outlet. Be aware that you can short-circuit or overheat the wiring.

It is very important that the cables are always in good condition. If there is one bulging or unprotected, it is best to replace them or cover them with an insulating tape.

5.Allow repairs to be done by an expert

Electrical repairs and renovations are tasks that should be carried out by an electrician. These professionals will guarantee that these jobs meet the established safety standards. It is best to leave things in the hands of certified people.

It is good to keep in mind that if at home you have an electrical appliance that shows failures or wear in its internal parts, it is best to replace it with a new one. Or you can seek the help of a professional who can repair the fault correctly.

To conclude, we recommend you hire a professional to perform electrical inspections throughout the home from time to time. Do not hesitate to replace appliances that are no longer safe. Keep in mind that you could be saving yourself more significant losses.

Take every precaution necessary and learn how to act in the face of a fire whose origin is an electrical defect. Remember that the main rule is you cannot pour water on it. In addition to having an evacuation plan, it is necessary to have a fire extinguisher at home.
