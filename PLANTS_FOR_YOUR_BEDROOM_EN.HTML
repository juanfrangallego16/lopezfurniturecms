IDEAL PLANTS FOR YOUR BEDROOM

There are those who think that having plants in the room is not recommended because at night they remove oxygen, but this is nothing more than a myth. It is true that plants, like humans and animals, absorb oxygen when they breathe. Let’s clear up the air and make it known that they do so in much smaller portions than people or pets. Because of this myth we stopped sleeping with them.
Keep in mind that plants produce oxygen during the day by photosynthesis, counteracting their nighttime respiration. So, if you were seriously wondering if it's bad to sleep with plants in the room, you now know the honest answer.
But if you still have certain doubts and the myth worries you, we have written this article specifically for you.
Let’s discover together which plants you can have in your room to enjoy a better rest.
There are plants that not only have a beautiful appearance and help to achieve a much more natural and aesthetic decoration, but also have a pleasant aroma that is very relaxing, and this helps you have a good night.
We must be clear that by themselves, plants will not cure insomnia or any other sleep disorder. However, they can add some well-being.
When selecting plants to decorate your room, it is important that you consider the type of care that each of them requires according to its species so that in this way they remain in good condition.
Due to the aromas or the purifying properties of their leaves, the following plants will help you create a wonderful environment in your room, and this help you sleep better.

1.JASMINE

Jasmine is an exotic plant you can safely have in your room if there is natural light. It has a slightly sweet aroma that has turned out to be very pleasant, creating relaxing sensations.

Something that you should keep in mind when selecting this plant is it need for abundant light. You should place it in a place where it can receive it all day long. In addition, it needs to be watered regularly.

The scent of jasmine is said to calm the body and mind. It has been shown to reduce anxiety, and its effect has nothing to do with the aroma, but rather with the white color of its petals, which transmits peace and tranquility. It is very suitable for a bedroom.

2.LAVENDER

Its aroma is very relaxing, and this makes it one of the best-known plants when it comes to reducing anxiety and inducing sleep. Its fragrance is credited with benefits such as regulating the heart rate, lowering blood pressure, and fighting stress. Many claim that its aroma can calm the crying of babies.

It should be noted that it is better known in the use of oils and special products, but sometimes we forget the easiest way to take advantage of it, and that is to have the lavender in a pot.

This plant is ideal for the bedroom. Its aroma is very popular because it can relax and induce sleep. It is not common to have it as an indoor plant, since the spaces do not lend themselves to it. However, there are those who buy or collect (in summer) bouquets to decorate and perfume their spaces.

3.ALOE VERA

There is no doubt that there are many reasons to have an Aloe Vera plant at home, since we can use it to treat the skin in case of burns.

But, in addition, it is listed by NASA as one of the main plants to improve the air, since it emits oxygen at night, and therefore promotes a more restful sleep. It can purify the air and removing toxins such as formaldehyde and benzene, which are often found in varnishes, floor cleaners, and detergents.

A very important point is that this is one of the easiest plants to care for. It requires a good source of natural light and regular watering will suffice. Remember that it does not require special fertilizers.

4.GARDENIAS

This plant is perfect for a bedroom. It emits an intense and pleasant perfume. This plant has its glossy leaves and white flowers. It brings beauty to the environment, although it also brings relaxation and delicacy due to the appearance it has and the aroma of flowers.

Keep in mind that this is a plant that requires good care. Not only constant watering, but it also needs a substrate rich in iron. It should also be placed near a light source.

They can be a bit difficult to maintain, but gardenias, with their glossy leaves and scented flowers, are popular to have in bedrooms as studies indicate that they help achieve better quality of sleep, even when comparing their effectiveness with that of Valium.

5.LILY OF PEACE

Its name can make us think that this plant should accompany us at night, and it is not far from reality. Because it is another plant that, according to NASA, purifies the air and helps filter harmful components such as benzene, trichlorethylene, and formaldehyde toxins.

In addition, the humidity that your flowers give off can increase the humidity in a room by up to 5%, and this is beneficial for sleeping as it suppresses airborne microbes that can cause allergies.

Now you know that with all the benefits that some plants bring, these can be your best friends at night. You just need to make the selection that best suits your style. Which of them would you choose for your room?
