5 TIPS FOR CREATING YOUR HOME OFFICE

We are aware that the circumstances we are going through today have forced most of the people to move the office to their homes. Others have had to reinvent themselves and start their own business from home.
Many are faced with the fact of having to deal with a completely new situation, having to develop their professional work from home, and facing the obstacles that are found in it.
It is very easy to give in to the temptation to work at the kitchen table, the dining room, or even from the sofa. But it is not a very good idea due to the issue of concentration and productivity, especially if you have children or other family members around. They can distract you from your task.
It's time to define and design a specific area so that you can comfortably and efficiently work: these 5 tips for creating your home office, will give you the key to creating your perfect space.
All those who at some point have had to work from home have faced these circumstances and many have had to implement a work routine at home to gain maximum concentration and in this way to be as productive as possible.
You may be wondering how can you make it work? With these 5 tips for creating your home office we will give you below, you will be a little closer to achieving your goal.

1. DEFINE THE WORKSPACE

An important point that you should keep in mind is that your workspace is away from televisions and away from people to avoid distractions and interruptions.

Any corner of your home can become a cozy place you can immerse yourself in your professional obligations.

A room divider is enough to create the space that often becomes a necessity. Playing with spaces and learning to take advantage of them allows the small spaces of a house or the narrow space under the stairs to be a comfortable workplace.

Workplaces at home are as important space as others. In them, we spend long periods of effort in which decoration, organization, and lighting can be key in our performance.

A space in which you can feel comfortable always brings benefits and the fact that it is the place of the house dedicated to work does not mean that it has to be boring.

2. TAKE THE LIGHTING INTO ACCOUNT

Something very simple and obvious, but sometimes due to the distribution of the spaces or the orientation of the home is not always possible. It is important to ensure that the workplace is near a window that lets in natural light, or so less, have good artificial lighting placed in a good position to avoid reflections on the computer screen to not strain your eyes. Lighting experts advise that your home workspace has more than one point of light.

Ultimately, good lighting in the workspace largely determines our productivity and contributes to a pleasant and comfortable work environment.

3. AROMATRIC COLORS AND SMELLS

To be calm and concentrated, it is important to be in an environment that is not color loud. The walls should be a color that motivates and inspires you when it comes to getting going and working.

It is also advisable to keep a chromatic harmony with the rest of the furniture and elements of your office. White never fails and helps the space look clean and uncluttered. The one that most favors concentration is blue and some details in red and orange stimulate creativity.

On the other hand, there are smells that also influence your mental activity and your mood. So you can play with aromatherapy: citrus aromas for memory and good spirits, mint for energy, and rosemary and jasmine for positivity and fatigue.

4. ERGONOMICS IS IMPORTANT

At this point, you should know that the computer must occupy the center position on the desk and be placed in front of you. If the desk is large enough to support the forearms and wrists you can place it to one side, trying to handle it without turning the neck. You must place the screen as far away from you as possible, at a minimum distance of 40 cm/15.75 inches.

Of course, a good desk and a good chair are essential. If you can, choose a desk-based on how you use the computer. Take into account whether you are going to spend a lot of time in front of it, talk on the phone, or make a lot of notes.

When choosing the chair, take into account an ergonomic model adjustable in height, with an adjustable backrest that adapts to the back, and is wide enough to protect your lumbar area. A good wheelbase will also help you move easily and prevent injury and muscle reloading.

5. A TOUCH OF GREEN

Did you know that placing a small plant on your desk can make you more productive? That's right, the simple presence of plants can bring you surprising benefits.

Living with fresh plants and flowers can help you feel healthier and more energetic to carry out your daily activities since it improves attention and concentration, causing you to become more productive. In addition, it makes your workspace much cooler and more beautiful.

Keep in mind that the migration from traditional work to “remote work”, without the necessary preparation or tools and without complying with ergonomic recommendations, can affect the conditions in which we work and with it our health. So it is good to apply these points and enjoy our daily work in a comfortable way.

We hope that these 5 tips for creating your home office will be of great help to you.
