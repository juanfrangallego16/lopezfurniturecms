IDEAS TO DECORATE A CHILDS ROOM

It is possible that one of the most difficult things when decorating children's rooms is combining functionality, necessity, and possibility. Especially when the spaces are not large or when they must be shared by our children. It is particularly important to let children play and that is why even the smallest rooms must have a small clear space for their fun.
We must be aware that children need a space to relax, play, and learn, a world of their own where they feel comfortable. That space is not only a gesture of love towards them, but also an adventure that connects us with our own childhood.
You could look at a child’s room from this point of view, children's bedrooms are those havens where children dream 24 hours: awake or asleep. Places of creation, play and pause, capable of enhancing skills and emotional resources or, on the contrary, limiting healthy development. Have you thought about it before?
If our children must share the room, we will have to use our imagination to make the most of the space. Ideally, if the dimensions of the room are large, we would be to be able to differentiate to some extent the place that each one occupies with two separate beds, two tables, and two cabinets to avoid arguments and to ensure that, even if they share it, each child has a certain sense of individuality.
Of course, if the room is small, we will have to prioritize practical utility and try to organize everything in the space we have and even so, not saturate the room to leave them a place to play comfortably. At this point is where solutions like the classic ones come into play like bunk beds, multifunctional, built-in, or convertible furniture.
As for child safety, there are many precautions to consider. There are especially dangerous elements that we must take care of, such as lamps, plugs, and corners. It is necessary to avoid placing table or floor lamps since they can be dangerous due to the plugs and the danger of these must also be avoided by placing protectors. As for the furniture in the room, we must try to have rounded corners to prevent our children from hurting themselves.
Decoration is an important element for each type of space. Your children's room is no exception. Having good decoration can stimulate and develop your children's creativity from an early age. That is why we give you some ideas to decorate a child's room.

1.The Walls

The first thing we recommend you do when decorating a child's bedroom is to paint the walls. Keep in mind that the choice of color is very important because it defines the identity of the little one.

The walls are part of the bedroom setting and play an important role in stimulating the sensory and creativity of children at an early age. The idea is to paint the bedroom and make it look nice and comfortable.

One of the most used decorative pieces are murals with animals or landscapes. They can show a certain personality of your child and match the themes you want to achieve. Remember to combine it with neutral colors such as white so that the piece does not lose prominence. Even if your child is a little older, the murals do not lose their validity.

Light colors are special for your children's rooms since they represent a color that is a trend for both girl’s and boy’s rooms. If you have a girl, you can decorate her room with pastel colors such as pink, purple, or lilac. For boys, you can use a natural green, blue, or orange color. Never use black to decorate your children's bedroom, as it can dull the environment and have negative effects on their emotional stability.

2.The Bed

Bunk beds are special for rooms with small spaces. In some children's rooms it is used with a double function, to sleep and as a play space. They go far beyond their usefulness, as they are part of the decorative space of the bedroom. It is one of the best solutions if you own a small house and have two or three children. Depending on the layout of the room, up to three beds can be placed accordingly. The most important thing is to give dynamism to the bedroom by combining cabins with versatile furniture such as shelves and desks. You can even be more creative and make a decorative fort or bridge.

3.Play Space

The real reality is that every home should have a play area. If you do not have a specific place in your home for this, you can put it together in the child's room. Remember that this is one of the most important spaces for your children, since here they will spend most of their time while they grow up. The first and most important thing is that this place has a specific theme with which your child feels identified to develop freely. Do not forget to put storage drawers or toy chest, as it is also important that your children do not have a visually contaminated space. Add colored rugs for a fun touch.

4.Place Decorative Vinyls

An excellent idea to decorate a child's room is decorative vinyl that gives it a lively appearance, with attractive colors that allow to capture the attention of your little one. In addition, these can be combined with the painting on the walls and decoration themes.

Finally, do not forget to place a sample of their personalized artwork, a drawing, or poem framed in a way that will make that room feel like his/her own and give them the confidence to continue with those creative activities.
