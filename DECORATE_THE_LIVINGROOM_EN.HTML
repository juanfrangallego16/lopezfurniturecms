5 STYLES TO DECORATE THE LIVING ROOM

Finding the ideal style for the living room can be a tiring job. Many times, we think that having just a few good ideas will be enough to guide us until we realize that we need to know more about the variety of styles in interior decoration that exist around the world. Today we will take a short walk through the art deco, industrial, Nordic, farmhouse or country house, and oriental styles.
We will share with you some details and characteristics of each of the styles mentioned before you start renovating your living room.
Remember that the space we live in is a true reflection of our personality. It reveals our feelings, the way we understand life.
Regardless of whether it is a photo, a chair, or a rug, everything around you has a reason. Home decor speaks of our personality.
All the objects that we have chosen, the intensity of the light, the color of the walls, even the smallest details of the kitchen, speak of our history and who we are and reveal who lives in that place.
There are many styles of decoration, and they all have something fascinating that attracts us. The hardest part is choosing! Today we propose to decorate your living room with design details that offer a positive change.

1.Art deco style

This is one of the most important interior design trends of the 20th century. Art Deco is indisputably a symbol of glamor and sophistication.

We must mention that within its fundamental characteristics is the trend towards geometric lines, in particular rectilinear lines, and monochrome palettes.

The key is to achieve a good contrast between light, dark, or bright colors, such as red, blue, or yellow.

You can never miss elements such as geometric prints in bright colors because these are spaces loaded with modern decor, that evoke an extravagant glamor. Dare to try it! This style will make you fall in love.

2.Industrial Style

This style is also known as urban; it is inspired by a warehouse or a loft in big cities.

A curious fact is that in the 50s, young people in search of growth in big cities like New York or Chicago had to start in places that years ago had been factories. So this means that beams, bricks and concrete were abundant.

In this way, this style was adopted, and today interior designers are inclined towards these elements in the decoration that proudly show the construction materials that others try to hide.

The furniture that is used in the decoration style is made of metal or iron and sometimes can be seen with a little rust on them. Untreated woods and veneers are also used.

There are two decorative pieces you cannot miss, paintings and photographs. They add a touch of color to an environment with opaque or pale colors.

To counteract coldness or dark tones, a great entrance of natural light is necessary.

3.Nordic Style

This style in recent years has become a trend that has gained a lot of strength over time. The origin of this type of decoration is in Norway, Sweden, Finland, and Denmark, where sunny days are scarce for much of the year, so a way was sought to replace that lack of light. The solution was found in light colors and some elements related to nature.

If you look at it carefully you will notice that the nature of the forest and mountains are the main inspiration for this style. You will see that in most cases there is furniture or wooden elements in the middle of light gray walls, similar color to the sky of those countries.

In addition to the colors, the furniture stands out for its simplicity and straight lines that give a distinctive touch to the space. In the rest of the decoration, green plants and flower patterns dominate. Although, lately the use of geometric figures such as squares, and vertical lines has been seen.

4.Farmhouse or country house Style

The intention of this style is to bring nature inside the home to create rural houses that transmit harmony, well-being, and comfort. If the house has old elements, it is important to maintain that character.

As you will see, these are mainly cool spaces with warm elements. In other words, walls in cold tones that provide luminosity, with warm colors. I think there is no better way to explain it.

Although stone and wood floors and furniture are favorites, there are a variety of natural materials that go very well with this style. The elements that are essential for this style are wood, stone, clay, wicker, and ceramic. Let your imagination fly and create the best country house style so that you can enjoy it as a family.

5.Oriental Style

We cannot deny that balance is the key in this type of decoration that has spread throughout the world, lovers of this style seek to create atmospheres of peace and balance in their homes.

Traditionally the predominant materials are bamboo, stone, rice paper, cedar wood, and silk. The colors of these elements are contrasted with the walls. In most cases are white or light tones, such as beige and some dare to add some red.

As you can see in this style there is a lot of similarity with minimalism, but there is usually a few more decorative elements or plants that create the feeling of tranquility and balance to the atmosphere.

Nature is important for this type of decoration. Artificial lighting is usually warm, in such a way that it resembles the rays of the sun, very in sync with the culture of the rising sun.
