HOW TO COMBINE GRAY TONES IN INTERIOR DESIGN

Gray is one of the basic colors to decorate with, but I would dare to say that it has turned out to be a very flattering neutral color and in addition, combining it with other colors is made easy. Do you want to learn how to combine it?

Gray tones are elegant, versatile, and can be perfectly adapted to a timeless interior design style. There is no doubt that knowing which colors combine perfectly with gray is essential for the decoration of your home to be a total success.

My intention with this article is to teach you how to combine gray tones with other neutrals, with intense colors, even with materials with warm textures that will help you give it all the prominence it deserves in your home.

1. GRAY TONES WITH EARTH TONES

There is no doubt that an excellent way to achieve more warmth is to combine gray with earth tones. Beige, mustard, camel, among others, are colors that blend perfectly with gray, regardless of whether they are in lighter or darker tones. Keep in mind, if we are attracted to this combination for our home, you can be sure that these colors will give a touch of warmth to your home.

Integrate them into your home decor in the proportions of your preference, either in furniture, curtains, or rugs. You can even add them in small details, like the accent pillow covers, lamps, or other accent pieces. In the case of dark browns, they are tones that we can combine with gray successfully. These colors go with gray so well, because they bring a lot of warmth to it, something that gray lacks.

In the case of the mustard color, I can say that it combines very well with gray. Even with dark gray! Yes, it is possible to be a little afraid that its decorative force will eat up the light and diminish the space, but I assure you that the mustard color can counteract this effect. Even mustard tones add vitality to gray.

When this combination is purely two-tone, in addition to being warm, it is striking and modern. It is also a good resource to compensate for the lack of light in gray and bring it to life. It should be noted that, with accent pillows, wall art, lamps, and other details you get that effect.

These tones have the great virtue of providing warmth without losing an iota of its sobriety. In addition to fabrics, we can also have this effect with natural fibers and leather. You can apply it on sofas or in small details that you add to the decoration.

2. GRAY AND BLACK

It can be said that gray is a color that is somehow part of black itself, and both lack any saturation, and it is for this reason that I cannot find fault with this logic. I also think that the combination of these two colors will always be a total success.

Naturally, it is necessary to be careful in the way we apply it, since we run the risk of creating environments that are too dark or very sober, since they lack chromatic tonality. I think that among the colors that combine with gray, black is an elegant and timeless option. Of course, it can't be just any gray, I recommended a medium gray and that the touches of black be very controlled; although, I am aware that this also varies according to the taste of everyone.

If you prefer, there is also the option of using different tones within the same range, and it can turn out to be a guaranteed success if we include more than two tones. Adding some chrome accessories could give your home a very urban result.

3. GRAY AND WHITE

It is a bright and natural combination, without complications. Combining gray with white results in a very well-matched couple that has been around for many years and will not go out of style.

It is awesome how bright the combination of dark gray with white can be. It is as if the white multiplied his powers. The result is modern! It is not a limited option, but can be applied in any space in the home, from the kitchen to the living room to the bedroom. It is undoubtedly a more current contrast.

This is a classic combination, but one that we could almost guarantee works in all styles. It is perfect because together they manage to stand out and define themselves within their subtleties. If you are looking for a color that matches gray, white never fails! In addition, it illuminates it and gives it freshness. Can you ask for anything better?

4. GRAY WITH RED, MAGENTA, OR PINK

In my opinion, one way to give life to the gray color would be to combine it with any warm tone. Although it may seem a bit crazy, red is perfect for this. You can add it in small proportions through smooth and patterned accent pillows. It is better when the pillows are combined with other light tones, such as beige or white.

This will surprise you, but another color that combines perfectly with gray is magenta. It will bring warmth to gray, but it also provides a more elegant and modern look. A key point so that the combination does not eat up all the light, is to accompany them with light colors such as white or beige.

Another key point is that when we opt for a light gray, we find the advantages and disadvantages of neutral tones such as white and beige, which need a contrasting color so that they are not so simple. This is when we see how good it is to combine gray with pink and with warm materials like wood.

5. GRAY AND BLUE

I am sure that blue, in its lighter and darker variants, is a good companion to gray. It can bring a lot of freshness if you select light shades, while if you go for darker shades, it accentuates the elegant and sophisticated character. Now, the effect can be a bit cold, so it doesn't hurt to add wooden elements that give it the touch of warmth it needs. You can include it in a large piece, such as the sofa in the living room or the headboard in your bedroom. Then it includes textiles in lighter shades of blue and gray. Donâ€™t forget to add elements in wood or natural fiber that provide warmth. Dark blue also works to give intensity without leaving the classic patterns to the combinations in the spaces of the home.

6. GRAY AND GREEN

If you like the combination of a light gray and a delicate and aquatic green, let me tell you that these colors are perfect with wooden details in light tones. This is a combination that will give your home design a light and enveloping effect, and it works perfectly if it is combined with natural wood.

If you want a more informal and youthful effect, try combining the color gray with pastel tones such as aqua green. It helps to soften it, take away its seriousness and you will get a modern result.

If you decide to decorate your home with gray tones, always keep in mind that the style changes completely if you want to combine a light or dark gray. Look at it this way, it can be cold when it starts to approach green and blue tones. Or warmer when it borders on beige and mustard tones.

Another important fact that you should not forget is that wood is perfect for ending this bad reputation that gray has for being too cold a color, and when it is combined a certain way, we give part of the prominence to wood.

To conclude, I think that one of the great virtues of gray tones is that they can be combined with practically any other color. Of course, it is the advantage of neutral tones. The effect achieved is different. Vibrant tones like red or turquoise will make it less serious and warm ones will bring light. But it also makes a very good pairing with the rest of the neutrals.
