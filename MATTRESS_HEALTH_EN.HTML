2.LET'S TALK ABOUT LATEX MATTRESSES

Latex can be found in two forms, natural or synthetic. Latex mattresses, in addition to being durable, are soft, flexible, and of course fit very well to the body because it distributes pressure evenly. In addition, the firmness of these mattresses is not affected by the ambient temperature.

You can be sure that when sleeping on one of these mattresses you will not feel an uncomfortable sinking sensation. If you are a person who suffers from allergies, with latex mattresses you won't have to worry because they are hypoallergenic. If you are allergic to latex itself, then this is not recommended for you.

They can get a bit pricey in comparison to your average mattress price. It is also known that a latex mattress has a bit of a rebound effect. These mattresses are heavy, so if you move frequently, you may find it somewhat uncomfortable each time you move. Also, latex mattresses should be laid on breathable bases, such as a box spring or slats. As I mentioned earlier, if you are allergic to latex, this mattress is not the one for you.

3.THE MEMORY FOAM MATTRESS

Memory foam is a highly adaptable material that was created by NASA to be used in rocket seat cushions. These were made to absorb the extreme pressure and excessive force astronauts experience during takeoff and re-entry.

Some of the advantages that I could mention of memory foam mattresses are that they mold perfectly to the body, while distributing your weight. This gives us better support for the spine and lower back.

Memory foam mattresses have a memory effect, these mattresses isolate movement and adapt according to body temperature.

If you are one of those people who move a lot when sleeping, a memory foam mattress is the best option for you especially if you have a partner who can feel all your movement. It will give you a good and your partner a much better sleep.

As a bonus, I would like to note that memory foam pillows are ideal for those who sleep on their back or side. It adapts to the neck and provides firmness and adaptability to the shape of the neck.

4.HYBRID SPRING MATTRESSES

Hybrid mattresses are known for their competitive price and advantages. There is no doubt that technology has contributed to making today's hybrid spring mattresses more resistant and durable.

They absorb weight individually because each spring is independent and located inside its own fabric bag. These mattresses are light and easy to handle and are also wonderful for summer days since they do not transmit heat.

There are low quality hybrid mattresses for the sake of affordability, but they typically don't last long because the springs lose support over time. The worst ones tend to make some noise, and this can be annoying.

5.FOAM MATTRESSES

Yes, it is a classic among classics. Despite its bad reputation, you can find them of good quality.

Some of their advantages are that they are made of a resistant and soft synthetic material, so they retain their original shape and of course they are much more durable than they seem when made well. In addition, its price is quite affordable.

But on the downside, they do not provide the firmness of a mattress with springs and low-quality ones barely perspire.


The ideal mattress for your bed must respect the natural curvature of the spine, and that depends on your weight, the position in which you sleep, and if you move a lot while you sleep.
