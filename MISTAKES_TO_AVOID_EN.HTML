7 MISTAKES TO AVOID IN THE KITCHEN

If you like to spend a lot of time in the kitchen, and sometimes find it difficult to maintain order and cleanliness in this space of your home, let me tell you that this article is made for you. Mainly it requires discipline and time, and you will see that once you set your mind to it, you will be able to achieve it. So, do not stop reading this article because we will tell you about 7 mistakes that we all make when tidying up the kitchen that you should avoid at all costs.
It's always a good time to deep clean your fridge, revitalize countertops, and rearrange kitchen drawers. It’s a good idea to observe every detail of the kitchen before starting. In this way you will know exactly where you should start. You can even create an organization system that is maintained over time.
We nt only want to mention the mistakes that are made when organizing the kitchen, we also want to share with you a possible solution.
When it comes time to organize the kitchen, one of the biggest mistakes is not completely emptying the cabinets and drawers. When we completely empty the drawers before we start rarranging the kitchen, we win in two ways: First, this allows us to classify the items into groups for better organization. Second, thisallows you to determine if you have unnecessary items that are taking up space.
Surely, many of us have done these mistakes and of course it is good to know how to avoid them. Here are 7 mistakes followed by the solution to keeping your kitchen clean and tidy once and for all.

1.STORING ITEMS ANYWHERE

This is one of the most common mistakes that many make, storing the elements in any free space, when the best would be where it is most practical. Whenever we talk about organization, it must be borne in mind that it is essential to store items by categories and similarities. This way it will be much easier to find things in the kitchen.

When we do not know how to take advantage of every corner of the kitchen, on many occasions this can generate a lot of disorder within it.

So, remember, first take everything out and take inventory of what you have. Once everything is categorized you can organize. Do not pile up and do not throw everything in the drwers. If you are organized, you will see how you will have space to spare.

2.USING THE DRAWERS FOR EVERYTHING

Each product, each tool in the kitchen has its function and, therefore, it must also have a suitable place to store it. You should avoid having a mess in the drawers. Which sadly is so common in many kitchens.

We know that everything that goes to this type of drawers ends up in oblivion on many occasions. We encourage you to try to reorganize it and it is possible that you will get a surprise with everything you had forgotten.

Drawers can reveal a lot about how we organize the kitchen. Remember that these areas are not made to pile everything. If you want to achieve optimal organization, the best thing is to remove what is in all the drawers, review and divide by categories.

3.INVESTING IN UNSUITABLE CONTAINERS

It is important to be clear about the functionality and practicality of the containers where we keep spices, sauces, and others. It can be tempting to buy a less expensive product, but in the end, it may be less functional and not actually meet our needs.

However, it is always best to invest in good quality containers that are useful, with safe lids, and environmentally friendly materials. Sometimes the cheapest ones can be made of a material whose life will be shorter and will protect our food less. Therefore, our recommendation is that it is better to buy glass containers instead of plastic ones.

4.IGNORING THE REFRIGERATOR

Yes, everything is behind closed doors. If it is crazy in the fridge if we do not open it, it is not visible. But we are aware that organizing the refrigerator can offer a better performance of it and a better display of the products you have in it.

Do not forget that when it comes to keeping the refrigerator organized, visibility is the key, an excellent idea is to use transparent and stackable containers to store leftovers and some transparent and labeled containers to put products such as cheese or meat. Of course, as you access the fridge every day, it is necessary that this be a continuous practice, so that order is maintained.

5.HAVING THE COUNTERS FULL OF THINGS

Failure to keep kitchen counters clear is a mistake that will take its toll. You start by leaving only a couple of things on top, and you end up with hardly any space to cook. Our recommendation is that you leave the counters free of utensils. If you do, you will see how your organization improves.

Remember, the countertops, kitchen tables, and/or bars should not be cluttered with things. Yes, it can be complicated if you live in the kitchen all day, so a good way to maintain order is to clean up when you finish in it. Tidy up if necessary and discard what you are no longer going to use.

6.NOT LOOKING FOR QUALITY

Quality is important both in the choice of containers for storing food and in organizing objects in the kitchen, from hangers to dividers. Choosing quality elements is relevant, even if we must make a greater financial investment.

Remember that in the end "Cheap is expensive.” Sometimes when appliances very cheap can be impractical and do not last long. That is why we recommend investing in quality and, over time, you will have to invest less in buying new models.

7.KEEPING TOO MANY APPLIANCES ON THE COUNTER

Of course, many of us love to have the Instant Pot, the coffee maker, even many could not live without their food processor. But the appliances can pile up on the kitchen counter and make it seem smaller than it really is.

Our recommendation in this case is that you store electrical appliances together in a closet instead, placing frequently used appliances front and center so they are easy to remove and put away again.
